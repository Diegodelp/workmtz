Al Dashboard for Gender violence en CongressWomen

Al Dashboard In response to the concerning prevalence of gender-based violence, we were commissioned by the National Congress to develop a Smart Board. This innovative tool tracks, classifies, and displays instances of online aggression directed towards congresswomen. By leveraging intelligent vectorization techniques on a comprehensive database, the Smart Board analyzes the language used to identify and categorize these gender-based attacks. This data visualization serves to raise public awareness regarding the significant issue of online harassment faced by female representatives.

IntelSocial

We serve as the primary AI service provider for IntelSocial (accessible via @intelsocialbot on Telegram). Our system ingests data from over 1,000 Argentinian newspapers and relevant social media platforms on a continuous basis. By applying multi-level, in-depth AI analysis, we develop innovative tools and indicators to enhance public discourse. One key capability is the detection of coordinated news dissemination (mono-editorialization) across media with similar editorial stances. Additionally, we generate heat maps visualizing geographical patterns of news coverage across Argentina. These regional patterns can reveal potential biases or trends related to specific events, such as surges in news related to insecurity or traffic accidents. Ultimately, our tools empower policymakers with data-driven insights to optimize public policies.

Al @ Cryptos

We explored the short-term cryptocurrency price prediction within the high-frequency trading domain using LSTM networks. We established error and uncertainty metrics to ensure the reliability of the predictions. Our findings revealed that the model could accurately predict price movements exceeding 3 standard deviations only if they were below the Binance fee. For larger variations, the accuracy significantly decreased, which we termed the "Market Uncertainty Principle." The most valuable outcome for the consortium was the confirmation of the limitations of the approach. This allowed them to make an informed decision to exit the cryptocurrency market at an opportune time.

Intelligent Socio-Economic Index

Socio-Economic map for Buenos Aires. We observed a non-random structure in responses to a comprehensive census survey in Buenos Aires, where all 79 questions per household exhibited a strong socioeconomic bias. Recognizing this underlying latent variable, likely representing socioeconomic status, we employed matrix factorization techniques to infer its continuous distribution across the city. This unsupervised approach yields the first continuous heatmap of socioeconomic levels for Argentinian cities, offering a crucial advantage in downstream analyses compared to traditional discrete or questionnaire-based methods.
